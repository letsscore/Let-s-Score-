Important concepts, trends and facts from the chapter.

Modern Periodic Table

Elements are arranged in increasing order of atomic number.

7 Periods and 18 Groups

Dobereiner's Triads

Groups of three elements with similar properties.

Example: Li, Na, K

Newlands' Law of Octaves

Every eighth element showed properties similar to the first element when arranged in increasing atomic mass.

Mendeleev's Periodic Table

Elements arranged according to increasing atomic masses.

Gaps were left for undiscovered elements.

Periods and Groups

  • Periods = Horizontal Rows
  • Groups = Vertical Columns
  • Periods = 7
  • Groups = 18

Periodic Trends

  • Atomic size decreases across a period.
  • Atomic size increases down a group.
  • Metallic character decreases across a period.
  • Metallic character increases down a group.
  • Valency changes systematically across periods.

Quick Facts

  • Modern periodic table is based on atomic number.
  • Noble gases are placed in Group 18.
  • Alkali metals belong to Group 1.
  • Halogens belong to Group 17.
  • Elements in the same group have similar chemical properties.
  • Atomic size is largest at the bottom of a group.
  • Metallic character decreases from left to right.
  • Mendeleev is known as the Father of the Periodic Table.

Important Data

  • Total Periods = 7
  • Total Groups = 18
  • Group 1 = Alkali Metals
  • Group 2 = Alkaline Earth Metals
  • Group 17 = Halogens
  • Group 18 = Noble Gases
