Carbon and Its Compounds

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the unique properties of carbon
  • Learn about covalent bonding in carbon compounds
  • Study versatile nature of carbon
  • Understand hydrocarbons and their types
  • Learn nomenclature of carbon compounds
  • Study chemical properties of carbon compounds
  • Understand ethanol and ethanoic acid
  • Learn about soaps and detergents

1. Introduction to Carbon

Carbon is a non-metal that forms a very large number of compounds due to its unique properties.

Carbon is present in coal, petroleum, natural gas, plants, animals and all living organisms.

2. Covalent Bonding

Carbon has four valence electrons and forms covalent bonds by sharing electrons.

Example:

Methane (CH₄)

In methane, carbon shares electrons with four hydrogen atoms.

3. Versatile Nature of Carbon

  • Catenation – ability of carbon atoms to form long chains
  • Tetravalency – carbon forms four covalent bonds
  • Forms stable compounds with many elements
  • Produces millions of organic compounds

4. Hydrocarbons

Compounds containing only carbon and hydrogen are called hydrocarbons.

Examples:

  • Methane (CH₄)
  • Ethane (C₂H₆)
  • Propane (C₃H₈)

5. Important Carbon Compounds

Ethanol (C₂H₅OH) is commonly used as a solvent and fuel.

Ethanoic Acid (CH₃COOH) is the main component of vinegar.

Ethanoic Acid + Ethanol → Ester + Water

6. Soaps and Detergents

Soaps are sodium or potassium salts of long-chain fatty acids.

Detergents clean effectively even in hard water.
