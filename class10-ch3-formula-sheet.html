Important reactions, concepts and facts from the chapter.

Reaction of Metals with Oxygen

Metal + Oxygen → Metal Oxide

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

Reaction of Metals with Water

Metal + Water → Metal Hydroxide + Hydrogen

2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂

Reaction of Metals with Acids

Metal + Acid → Salt + Hydrogen Gas

Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂

Displacement Reaction

More reactive metal displaces a less reactive metal from its salt solution.

Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu

Reactivity Series

K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Pb > H > Cu > Ag > Au

Quick Facts

  • Metals are lustrous, malleable and ductile.
  • Non-metals are generally brittle and poor conductors.
  • Metals form positive ions (cations).
  • Non-metals form negative ions (anions).
  • Copper and silver are excellent conductors of electricity.
  • Gold is the least reactive metal.
  • Corrosion is the gradual destruction of metals.
  • Rusting is the corrosion of iron.
  • Galvanisation prevents rusting.
