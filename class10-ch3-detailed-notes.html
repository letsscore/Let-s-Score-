Metals and Non-Metals

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand physical properties of metals and non-metals
  • Learn chemical properties of metals
  • Study reactions of metals with oxygen, water and acids
  • Understand reactivity series
  • Learn extraction and corrosion of metals

1. Metals and Non-Metals

Elements are broadly classified into metals and non-metals based on their physical and chemical properties.

Examples:

Metals: Iron, Copper, Aluminium, Zinc

Non-Metals: Oxygen, Sulphur, Carbon, Nitrogen

2. Physical Properties of Metals

  • Lustrous (shiny)
  • Malleable
  • Ductile
  • Good conductors of heat and electricity
  • Generally hard and strong

3. Physical Properties of Non-Metals

  • Dull appearance
  • Brittle in nature
  • Non-ductile
  • Poor conductors of heat and electricity
  • Generally soft

4. Chemical Properties of Metals

Metals react with oxygen to form metal oxides.

Example:

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

Metals also react with dilute acids to produce hydrogen gas.

Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂

5. Reactivity Series

The arrangement of metals in decreasing order of reactivity is called the reactivity series.

K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Pb > H > Cu > Ag > Au
