Important formulas, reactions and concepts from the chapter.

Neutralisation Reaction

Acid + Base → Salt + Water

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

Reaction of Acid with Metal

Acid + Metal → Salt + Hydrogen Gas

Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂

Reaction of Acid with Metal Carbonate

Acid + Carbonate → Salt + Water + Carbon Dioxide

Na₂CO₃ + 2HCl → 2NaCl + H₂O + CO₂

Reaction of Acid with Metal Hydrogen Carbonate

NaHCO₃ + HCl → NaCl + H₂O + CO₂

pH Scale

  • pH < 7 → Acidic
  • pH = 7 → Neutral
  • pH > 7 → Basic

Quick Facts

  • Acids produce H⁺ ions in aqueous solution.
  • Bases produce OH⁻ ions in aqueous solution.
  • Litmus turns red in acids and blue in bases.
  • Phenolphthalein is colourless in acids and pink in bases.
  • pH scale ranges from 0 to 14.
  • Common salt is NaCl.
  • Baking soda is NaHCO₃.
  • Washing soda is Na₂CO₃·10H₂O.
