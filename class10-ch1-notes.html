Chemical Reactions and Equations

1. Chemical Reaction

A chemical reaction is a process in which one or more substances (reactants) are converted into new substances (products) having different properties.

Examples:

Burning of magnesium ribbon

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

2. Characteristics of Chemical Reactions

  • Evolution of gas
  • Change in colour
  • Change in temperature
  • Formation of precipitate
  • Change in state

3. Chemical Equations

A chemical equation represents a chemical reaction using symbols and formulae.

Hydrogen + Oxygen → Water

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

4. Balanced Chemical Equation

A balanced equation has equal number of atoms on both sides.

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

5. Types of Chemical Reactions

Combination Reaction

Two or more substances combine to form one product.

CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂

Decomposition Reaction

One compound breaks into simpler substances.

2AgCl → 2Ag + Cl₂

Displacement Reaction

A more reactive element displaces a less reactive element.

Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu

Double Displacement Reaction

Exchange of ions between compounds.

Na₂SO₄ + BaCl₂ → BaSO₄ + 2NaCl

6. Oxidation

Addition of oxygen or removal of hydrogen is called oxidation.

7. Reduction

Removal of oxygen or addition of hydrogen is called reduction.

8. Corrosion

Slow destruction of metals due to atmospheric action.

Example: Rusting of iron.

9. Rancidity

Oxidation of fats and oils causing unpleasant smell and taste.

Chapter Summary

  • Chemical reactions form new substances.
  • Equations represent reactions.
  • Balanced equations obey conservation of mass.
  • Main reaction types: Combination, Decomposition, Displacement, Double Displacement.
  • Corrosion affects metals.
  • Rancidity affects food materials.
