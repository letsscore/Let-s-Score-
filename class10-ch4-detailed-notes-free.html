Carbon and Its Compounds

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the unique properties of carbon
  • Learn covalent bonding
  • Study hydrocarbons and their classification
  • Understand homologous series
  • Learn about ethanol and ethanoic acid

1. Carbon

Carbon is a versatile element that forms a large number of compounds.

Its atomic number is 6.

Carbon shows tetravalency and catenation.

2. Covalent Bonding

Carbon shares electrons with other atoms to form covalent bonds.

Example:

Methane (CH₄)

3. Hydrocarbons

Compounds containing only carbon and hydrogen are called hydrocarbons.

  • Saturated Hydrocarbons (Alkanes)
  • Unsaturated Hydrocarbons (Alkenes and Alkynes)

4. Homologous Series

A series of compounds with the same functional group and similar chemical properties is called a homologous series.

Successive members differ by -CH₂.

5. Ethanol and Ethanoic Acid

Ethanol (C₂H₅OH) is an alcohol.

Ethanoic Acid (CH₃COOH) is commonly known as acetic acid.
