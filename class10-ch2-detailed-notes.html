Acid bases and Salt

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand acids, bases and salts
  • Learn indicators and their uses
  • Study chemical properties of acids and bases
  • Understand pH scale and its importance
  • Learn preparation and uses of important salts

1. Acids and Bases

Acids are substances that produce hydrogen ions (H⁺) in aqueous solution, while bases produce hydroxide ions (OH⁻) in aqueous solution.

Examples:

Acids: HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃

Bases: NaOH, KOH, Ca(OH)₂

Acids generally taste sour whereas bases are bitter and soapy in nature.

2. Indicators

  • Litmus Solution
  • Phenolphthalein
  • Methyl Orange
  • Turmeric Indicator
  • China Rose Indicator

Indicators help us identify whether a substance is acidic or basic.

3. The pH Scale

The pH scale is used to measure the acidic or basic nature of a solution.

  • pH < 7 → Acidic
  • pH = 7 → Neutral
  • pH > 7 → Basic

4. Salts

Salts are formed when an acid reacts with a base in a neutralisation reaction.

Example:

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
