Periodic Classification of Elements

1. Need for Classification

As the number of known elements increased, scientists felt the need to arrange them systematically according to their properties.

Classification helps in studying elements easily and predicting their properties.

2. Dobereiner's Triads

Johann Wolfgang Dobereiner grouped elements into sets of three called triads.

Example:

Lithium (Li), Sodium (Na), Potassium (K)

The atomic mass of the middle element was approximately the average of the other two.

3. Newlands' Law of Octaves

John Newlands arranged elements in increasing order of atomic mass.

He observed that every eighth element had properties similar to the first element.

4. Mendeleev's Periodic Table

Mendeleev arranged elements in increasing order of atomic masses and grouped elements with similar properties together.

He left gaps for undiscovered elements and predicted their properties.

5. Modern Periodic Table

The modern periodic table is based on increasing atomic number.

Elements are arranged in:

  • 7 Periods (Horizontal Rows)
  • 18 Groups (Vertical Columns)

6. Periodic Trends

  • Atomic size decreases across a period
  • Atomic size increases down a group
  • Valency changes systematically
  • Metallic character decreases across a period
  • Metallic character increases down a group
