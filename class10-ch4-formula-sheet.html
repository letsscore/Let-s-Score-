Important reactions, concepts and facts from the chapter.

Covalent Bonding

Carbon shares electrons to complete its octet.

Example: Methane (CH₄)

Homologous Series

Successive members differ by a –CH₂ group.

Methane → Ethane → Propane → Butane

Combustion Reaction

Hydrocarbon + Oxygen → Carbon Dioxide + Water + Heat

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + Heat

Oxidation of Ethanol

Ethanol on oxidation forms Ethanoic Acid.

CH₃CH₂OH + [O] → CH₃COOH + H₂O

Esterification Reaction

Ethanoic Acid + Ethanol → Ester + Water

CH₃COOH + C₂H₅OH → CH₃COOC₂H₅ + H₂O

Saponification Reaction

Ester + Sodium Hydroxide → Soap + Alcohol

This reaction is used in the manufacture of soaps.

Quick Facts

  • Carbon has four valence electrons.
  • Carbon exhibits tetravalency.
  • Catenation is the ability of carbon to form chains.
  • Compounds containing only carbon and hydrogen are called hydrocarbons.
  • Saturated hydrocarbons contain single bonds.
  • Unsaturated hydrocarbons contain double or triple bonds.
  • Ethanol is used as a fuel and solvent.
  • Ethanoic acid is the main component of vinegar.
  • Soap molecules form micelles during cleaning.
  • Detergents work effectively in hard water.

Important Formulae

  • Methane – CH₄
  • Ethane – C₂H₆
  • Propane – C₃H₈
  • Butane – C₄H₁₀
  • Ethanol – C₂H₅OH
  • Ethanoic Acid – CH₃COOH
