Important formulas, reactions and concepts from the chapter.

Tetravalency of Carbon

Carbon has valency 4 and forms four covalent bonds.

Example: CH₄ (Methane)

Covalent Bonding

Carbon shares electrons with other atoms to complete its octet.

Example: CH₄, C₂H₆, H₂O

Saturated Hydrocarbons

Contain only single bonds between carbon atoms.

General Formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂

Example: CH₄, C₂H₆

Unsaturated Hydrocarbons

Contain double or triple bonds.

Alkenes: CₙH₂ₙ

Alkynes: CₙH₂ₙ₋₂

Important Compounds

Ethanol = C₂H₅OH

Ethanoic Acid = CH₃COOH

Quick Facts

  • Carbon shows catenation.
  • Carbon forms covalent compounds.
  • Hydrocarbons contain only carbon and hydrogen.
  • Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons.
  • Alkenes contain double bonds.
  • Alkynes contain triple bonds.
  • Ethanol is an alcohol.
  • Ethanoic acid is commonly called acetic acid.
  • Soap molecules have hydrophilic and hydrophobic ends.
